What is Summer Research Institute?

Among those interested in pursuing research opportunities, students often consider/ participate in SRI which stands for Summer Research Institute. SRI gives students the opportunity to conduct paid research for 10 weeks under the supervision of a mentor which can either be on campus or off campus. Such possible mentors include faculty from Barnard or Columbia and mentors from Mt. Sinai, NYU, the New York Botanical Garden, etc. As for eligibility, rising sophomores, juniors, and seniors graduating fall term at Barnard College are eligible to participate in SRI. Some possible departments students may conduct research for include biology, chemistry, cognitive science, computer science, environmental science, mathematics and statistics, neuroscience and behavior, physics and astronomy, and psychology. Lastly, the general steps to participate in SRI includes filling out an interest form, finding a research mentor, securing confirmation of mentor interest, and completing the SRI application.
